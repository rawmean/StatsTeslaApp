What’s New

What’s New (v6.2.33)

FSD Utilization

  • You can now see a distribution of FSD utilization across users in the "Compare" tab of the app.
  • If your car has FSD and HW4, you can also see how your FSD utilization compares to other users in the same graph.
  • If your car has FSD and HW4, your FSD utilization also is displayed in the main screen of the app.

What’s New (v6.2.30)

Adjust Equalizer

  • You can use this feature to adjust the equalizer setting for optimal listening experience.
  • The app plays some tones and records the frequency response of the cabin. Then adjusts the equalizer settings to compensate and achieve an optimal setting.
  • This feature is in the Settings tab under "Tools".

What’s New (v6.2.28)

Sending the Car to Nearest ER

  • You can now ask the car to take you to the nearest ER.
  • This uses the new Siri interaction that has been added to Stats app recently.

What’s New (v6.2.26)

Heads-up Display

  • A full-screen Heads-up Display designed for driving that shows your current speed, acceleration, heading, and speed limit in real-time.
  • Also, has a mirror-mode for windshield reflection projection at night.

What’s New (v6.2.21)

Talk to Siri Using Natural Language

  • Say "Siri, ask Stats" and when Siri responds, you can ask questions about your car(s) and send commands to the car.

What’s New (v6.2.20)

Battery Temperature History

  • You can now track the battery temperature history, along with inside and outside temperature, on the main tab.

What’s New (v6.2.15)

Temperature History

  • You can now track the inside and outside temperature history of the car on the main tab.
  • Observe how inside temperature can rise above outside temperature and how quickly venting windows or turning on the AC can reduce the temperature.

What’s New (v6.1.62)

Watch Shake

  • You can shake your watch while near the car to perform the following actions automatically without launching the app.
  • Open the trunk.
  • Open the frunk.
  • Open the charge port, even when the car is asleep.
  • Unlatch the driver or passenger door.
  • This feature uses bluetooth and works without internet connectivity.
  • To enable this feature: make sure the watch is paired with your car, launch Stats on your watch, select your car, tap on settings, and select the action associated with shaking the watch.

What’s New (v6.1.59)

Wake Times

  • This card, in the second tab, shows the timestamps when your car was woken up.
  • For example, the chart can show that the car was woken up twice today.
  • Note: Stats app does not wake the car without user interaction, not even when you launch the app.

What’s New (v6.1.57)

Low Battery Range and Tesla Firmware Info

  • When the Estimated Range is below 100km (62mi), you can see how far you can travel on the map.
  • Tap on a Tesla firmware bar to see what is new in that firmware.

What’s New (v6.1.52)

Battery Voltage and Battery Temperature

  • This card in the second tab shows the difference between the minimum and maximum battery voltages and the ID of the corresponding brick.
  • In a healthy battery, the difference between minimum and maximum voltage should be small.
  • Now you can also see the live battery temperature on the main screen of the app. This is the average across all battery modules.

What’s New (v6.1.51)

CyberTruck Filter, Sentry Notification, and Live Charge Activity

  • The new filter for CyberTruck updates firmware histograms as well as efficiency and phantom drain histograms for CyberTrucks.
  • When the car detects a sentry event, a notification is issued. Tapping on the notification takes you to Tesla app to view live cameras or watch the recorded sentry clip.
  • While the car is charging, launch the app on iPhone to start the Live Charge Activity. You will see the charging status on the Watch as well as the Dynamic Island of the iPhone and the Lock Screen.

What’s New (v6.1.32)

Control Center Widgets

  • Now you can monitor the car's state and interact with it directly from the Control Center without launching any app.
  • Supports multiple cars. After adding the widget, tap on it to configure.

What’s New (v6.1.26)

Auto Unlatch

  • The exclusive new Auto Unlatch feature unlatches the driver door automatically when you approach the car using your watch.
  • The feature does not unlatch if the car is unlocked already. This is to avoid unlatching when the car is parked in your garage every time you visit the garage.
  • To enable this feature, launch Stats on your watch, select your car, tap on settings, and enable the switch shown below.

What’s New (v6.1.19)

Smart Venting

  • The new Smart Venting feature is smarter now.
  • If you configure the feature to ask you for confirmation, it can learn what to do for the current car location.
  • For example, if the location is safe, the app can automatically vent the windows when inside temperature exceeds your configured limit and the car is parked around that location.

What’s New (v6.1.17)

AutoNav

  • The new AutoNav feature predicts your destination based on your trip history, current time, and day of the week.
  • Tap on the car console to start the navigation and view ETA or alternative routes.

What’s New (v6.1.2)

Charge Reminders, Weekly Distance Charts, and Tesla News Widget

  • The app analyzes your driving pattern and, if your current Estimated Range is less than the range that you might need tomorrow, it will remind you to charge the car.
  • This is in addition to the charge reminder based on the SoC limit that you can set.
  • In the second tab of the app, you will also see a new bar chart that indicates the typical distance that you travel on each day of the week.
  • A new Home Widget presents Tesla headline news and lets you open each story in your default browser.

What’s New (v6.1.1)

Multiple-door Unlatch from Apple Watch

  • You can configure Stats on your watch to unlatch more than one door simultaneously.
  • Any configuration of front and rear doors is possible.
  • Activate it using the widget on your watch face or using the button in Stats app.

What’s New (v6.0)

Official Tesla API

  • Stats app uses the new official Tesla API now.
  • The official Tesla API is more secure and more reliable.
  • Stats app now appears in your Tesla account under "Third Party Apps".
  • Stats app is among the first apps that switched to the Official Tesla API ahead of the Jan 2024 deadline set by Tesla.

What’s New (v5.8.1)

Watch Complications

  • You can configure the corner and center complications to show % SoC instead of range.
  • To do this, head to the settings screen inside Stats app on your watch.

What’s New (v5.8)

Interactive Widgets, Proximity Activation, and Brand New Watch App

  • Interactive widgets require iOS 17. Tap twice on buttons to interact without launching the app. Even the Lock Screen action widgets no longer open the app.
  • Frunk or trunk Proximity Activation opens the chosen compartment automatically when you approach the car after tapping the watch complication.
  • A brand new watch app adds several complications, interaction buttons, and more info.
  • The firmware histograms can now be sorted based on the FSD version of the firmware.
  • The app connects to the car much faster now. Even the Today Widget is optimized and shows car info when the car is asleep.

What’s New (v5.7.21)

Trip Export and Tax Report

  • You can now export your trips and the app also generates a report that you can use to determine your tax deduction associated with car trips.

What’s New (v5.7.20)

Ask Questions from the Tesla Manual

  • You can ask questions from the Tesla manual and get answers from different sections of Tesla manuals put together using GPT technology.

What’s New (v5.5.20)

Smart Venting

  • Stats app will vent the windows if inside temperature exceeds the temperature that you specify.
  • It also closes the windows once temperature goes below what you specify.
  • You can require a confirmation or you can let the app do this automatically.
